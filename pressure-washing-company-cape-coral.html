Pressure Washing vs. Soft Washing

Professional pressure washing companies typically offer two distinct cleaning methods:

Pressure Washing: Uses high-pressure water (2,500-4,000 PSI) to blast away tough stains, dirt, and grime from durable surfaces like concrete, brick, and stone. This method is ideal for driveways, sidewalks, parking lots, and other hard surfaces that can withstand high pressure.

Soft Washing: Employs lower pressure (less than 500 PSI) combined with specialized cleaning solutions to safely clean delicate surfaces like roofs, siding, windows, and painted surfaces. The cleaning agents do most of the work, breaking down organic growth and stains, while the low pressure rinses away the debris without causing damage.

Factors Affecting Pricing

Several variables influence the final cost of pressure washing services:

Size and Scope

Larger properties and more extensive cleaning projects naturally cost more. Most companies price based on square footage or linear footage for fences and driveways.

Level of Contamination

Heavily soiled surfaces with severe mold, mildew, or algae growth require more time, stronger cleaning solutions, and potentially multiple treatments. Properties that haven't been cleaned in years will cost more than those maintained regularly.

Surface Type

Different materials require different approaches. Delicate surfaces that require soft washing and specialized cleaning solutions may cost more than simple concrete cleaning. Wood surfaces often require additional care and specific treatments.

Accessibility

Hard-to-reach areas, second stories, steep roofs, and properties with challenging access increase labor time and equipment needs, affecting cost. Properties requiring special equipment like boom lifts or scaffolding will have higher prices.

Services beyond basic cleaning, such as sealing, staining, or applying protective coatings, add to the total cost but provide additional value and longer-lasting results.

Common Pressure Washing Mistakes to Avoid

Whether you're hiring a professional or attempting DIY pressure washing, avoid these common mistakes that can damage your property:

1. Using Excessive Pressure

More pressure isn't always better. Excessive pressure can gouge wood, etch concrete, crack vinyl siding, force water under siding causing mold, and destroy roof shingles. Professional companies know the appropriate pressure for each surface.

2. Wrong Nozzle Selection

Pressure washer nozzles deliver water at different spray patterns and pressures. Using the wrong nozzle can cause damage or ineffective cleaning. Professionals have extensive nozzle selections and know which to use for each application.

3. Holding the Wand Too Close

Maintaining proper distance from the surface is crucial. Too close, and you risk damage; too far, and cleaning becomes ineffective. This skill comes with experience and training.

4. Ignoring Safety Precautions

Pressure washers are powerful tools that can cause serious injury. High-pressure water can penetrate skin, cause cuts, and lead to infections. Working on ladders while operating equipment is particularly dangerous.

5. Using Incorrect Cleaning Solutions

Not all cleaning solutions work on all surfaces. Using the wrong product can damage surfaces, kill plants, or simply be ineffective. Professional companies use specifically formulated products for different applications.

6. Overlooking Proper Surface Preparation

Effective cleaning requires proper preparation—covering landscaping, closing windows, protecting electrical outlets, and pre-treating tough stains. Skipping these steps leads to subpar results or damage.

7. Inconsistent Technique

Creating streaks, tiger stripes, or uneven cleaning is a common DIY mistake. Professional technicians use systematic techniques that ensure even, consistent results across entire surfaces.

8. Damaging Landscaping

Pressure washing runoff can contain chemicals that harm plants and landscaping. Professionals protect vegetation and properly manage runoff to prevent damage.
